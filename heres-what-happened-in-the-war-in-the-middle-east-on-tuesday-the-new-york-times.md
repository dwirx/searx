# Here’s What Happened in the War in the Middle East on Tuesday - The New York Times

Source: https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/17/world/middleeast/iran-war-israel-middle-east-recap.html

The deaths of two top Iranian officials were announced on Tuesday, while President Trump once again lashed out at NATO allies who refused to aid the U.S. war effort against Iran.

Israel killed two of Iran’s top leaders on Tuesday: Ali Larijani, the head of the country’s Supreme National Security Council, and Brig. Gen. Gholamreza Soleimani, the head of a powerful plainclothes militia aligned with the Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps.

Iranian state media confirmed both deaths. Mr. Larijani was a close confidant of Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, the supreme leader who was killed at the start of the war. Perceived as an influential pragmatist with the clout to negotiate with the United States, Mr. Larijani’s killing may embolden even more hard-line Iranian leaders, as President Trump struggles to get help from allies in mitigating the economic impacts of the war and with divides over the conflict in his party.

President Trump on Tuesday lashed out again at NATO allies who had rebuffed his attempts to get help to open the Strait of Hormuz, a key global shipping lane where ships have been attacked, sending oil prices surging. And he minimized the resignation of a senior administration official, Joe Kent, the director of the National Counterterrorism Center, who quit over the Iran war in a letter on Tuesday, calling Mr. Kent “weak” on security.

Iran: The funeral procession for Mr. Larijani and his son, Morteza Larijani, who was also killed, will be held on Wednesday, along with the public funeral of navy officers from the IRIS Dena, which was sunk by an American submarine off the coast of Sri Lanka, the Fars News agency said. The Iranian Red Crescent Society said Tuesday that 236 health care sites have been “severely damaged” and 498 schools have been struck in U.S.-Israeli attacks, 17 Red Crescent centers have been hit, 35 ambulances taken out of service, and one relief worker killed. The report did not provide an overall death toll.

Lebanon: The Israeli military issued a large-scale evacuation warning late on Tuesday for the southern Lebanese city of Tyre and surrounding towns — the first of its kind there during the war and a signal that a major military operation is likely imminent. The Israeli military said it had deployed more troops in southern Lebanon for “limited and targeted ground operations” against Hezbollah, the Iran-backed militia. The Lebanese health ministry said that more than 900 people have been killed and over 2,200 injured since the conflict between Israel and Hezbollah reignited.

Persian Gulf: An Iraqi military spokesman, Sabah al-Numan, said that Iran-backed militia groups had again attacked the American Embassy in Baghdad on Tuesday evening, after similar incidents earlier in the day. A tanker anchored near a port in the United Arab Emirates was hit by a projectile early Tuesday, sustaining minor damage, in the first strike on a ship in and around the Strait of Hormuz in five days, according to United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations. At least 17 vessels have been attacked in the region since the U.S.-Israeli war in Iran began.

Israel: The Israeli military said on Tuesday it had struck command centers belonging to Hezbollah in the Lebanese capital and targeted “launch sites.” In Israel, local government officials warned residents near the Lebanon border to remain vigilant ahead of what the Israeli military said might be an expansion of rocket fire by the militant group on Tuesday, following the killings of top Iranian leaders.

Resignation: Joe Kent, director of the National Counterterrorism Center, became the first senior administration official to resign over the war, and a symbol of the rifts in the Republican Party between interventionists and those who oppose U.S. involvement in foreign affairs. “I cannot in good conscience support the ongoing war in Iran,” he wrote in a letter to Mr. Trump. “Iran posed no imminent threat to our nation, and it is clear that we started this war due to pressure from Israel and its powerful American lobby,” he said.

Shipping and oil: The United Nations food agency said the U.S.-Israeli war on Iran threatened to drive up the number of people suffering from acute hunger by tens of millions worldwide. Disruption of shipping lanes amid the war is already delaying lifesaving food aid deliveries. Shipping costs have risen 18 percent. And higher oil prices are driving up the agency’s operating costs, said Carl Skau, the World Food Program’s deputy executive director and operations chief. Brent crude, the international oil benchmark, continued to inch up Tuesday, settling at $103.42 per barrel, up 3.2 percent for the day and over 42 percent since the war began.

Ephrat Livni is a Times reporter covering breaking news around the world. She is based in Washington.
